About Me

Tessa Bennion is an academic painter working primarily in oil, charcoal, and graphite. Her practice balances direct observation with long-form studio work, building surfaces slowly—glaze over glaze—until form feels inevitable.

She draws inspiration from classical ateliers, museum study, and the rhythms of everyday life. Portraits, still life arrangements, and plein air studies share a common thread: clear value structure, restrained color, and a quiet attention to edges.

When not at the easel, Tessa teaches foundational drawing and keeps a relentless sketch habit. This site is a living catalog of finished works, process notes, and the drawings that knit it all together.
